    • Hi! My name is Maud and I work as a research scientist (postdoctoral fellow) in the field of experimental psychology, with a focus on mental health, wellbeing and decision-making. In 2021 I want to start a new career journey as a data analyst / data scientist and use my analytical skills to support evidence-based practices and drive positive change.

    I have always been a very curious person. This is what originally led me to science and is why I am passionate about travelling and exploring different cultures and cuisines. I particularly enjoy using data analysis and data visualization to translate complex questions into understandable insights. This is not only relevant in science, but also to help businesses make strategic decisions and address social challenges. Thanks to doing both work-related and personal projects, I am constantly strengthening my skills in statistical analysis and reproducible data visualization and reporting.

    As an experimental psychologist, I have 10 years of experience in doing empirical research and applying different statistical analysis methods to answer questions related to mental wellbeing and stress regulation, as well as fundamental interactions between cognition and emotion. For example, by using empirical data, I investigate how individual differences in cognitive processes and biases in the way we process emotional information can predict changes in anxiety over time. Additionally, I am interested in how the experience of anxiety and depressive symptoms influences decision-making. Many of the decisions we make in daily life are influenced by the potential outcomes that are associated with different choice options. I investigate whether individual differences in the experience of anxiety and depressive symptoms influences how people use reward- and risk-related information to achieve desired outcomes.

    To find out more about my research work, see my publications.
